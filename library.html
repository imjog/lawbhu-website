About Library

Library is an important part of an academic institution of higher learning. Its importance increases as it plays a significant role in pursuit of academic excellence. Keeping this in view, a constant effort is being made to enrich the library with collection of a wide variety of books and journals from the point of law students and scholars. The Library regale to the needs of students and researchers engaged in legal studies and intensive research, besides catering to the needs of the Bench and the Bar.

Law school library occupies the whole of first floor of the institution with a student capacity of over 100 students at a time. The Law School has around eighty thousand books and more than 135 international and national journals. Facilities of Internet through LAN & Wi-Fi known as 'Cyber Judice' is also added to law library. Open access system is adopted for an easy access to literature and other information. Library provides 24*7 E-resources access to its students on and off the campus. Library has the collection to judgments, reporters, manuscripts and law reviews of national and international levels. Several important reference and citation analysis sources are also being subscribed by the University. Users may contacts the librarian for any additional help required while accessing the databases. It uses URKUND as a plagiarism detection tool provided by INFLIBNET.

For further references students can access Central Library both online and offline. Named after Sayaji Rao Gaekwad, Central Library is one magnificent building of resources. the library took long strides during sixties and seventies in its development and metamorphosed in a system of libraries with the establishment of institute, faculty and departmental libraries during the period. Presently the Banaras Hindu University Library System consists of Central Library at apex and 3 Institute Libraries, 8 Faculty Libraries, 25 Departmental Libraries, with a total collection of over 13 lakh volumes to serve the students, faculty members, researchers, technical staff of fourteen faculties consisting of 126 subject departments of the university.
